New Valley

major general mahmoud ashmawe, governor of the new valley, discussed with the delegation of the egyptian food bank foundation the procedures for the start of the giant project in the province, which aims to develop a number of villages in the province to bring about positive change and empowerment in the lives of the inhabitants of these villages.

the province's needs for village development were presented, and the achievements of the giant project, through which 23 of the 100 villages were developed over 10 years.

shirin akl, director of the project department, said the villages will be selected based on their basic needs and demands for the development and improvement of services in these villages, noting that a protocol of cooperation with the province will be signed soon to implement the project.

she explained that the activities of the project in these villages will include the construction, maintenance and equipment of schools, health and veterinary units in the villages and target areas, as well as attention to the infrastructure of water delivery, paving roads and lighting, as well as attention to the human element in terms of vocational, professional and administrative training for young people as well as various awareness campaigns, medical convoys and convoys of good.

the governor stressed that it is time to join all efforts to eradicate poverty and provide basic needs for citizens, stressing the importance of the participation of civil society institutions and private development partners with the executive bodies of the province to develop and improve life in the villages most in need in the fields of infrastructure and human development, and remove obstacles to the implementation of development plans and projects.
